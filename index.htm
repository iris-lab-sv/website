BIO:

She received her Ph.D. in Computer Science from North Carolina State University in 2010. Her main research is network and system security with an emphasis on malware detection, insider attacks, botnets, traceback to detect attacks. Currently, she is focusing on security issues in Smart Grid, Software Defined Networks, Big Data, internet of Things. Before joining San Jose State University in August, 2013, she was a post-doctoral researcher in the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. She was also a post-doctoral research scientist in the Computer Science at Columbia University in New York City in 2011.
She has been a programming committee or session chair for many peer-reviewed IEEE conferences. She holds the Kordestani Endowed Chair in the College of Engineering at SJSU in 2016. She is working on several industry projects in Silicon Valley.
